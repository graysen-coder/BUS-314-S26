## Executive Summary

Microsoft Corporation operates across cloud computing, enterprise software, and consumer technology — three sectors where capital efficiency and margin discipline are paramount competitive differentiators. This memo frames a four-stage accounting ratio analysis of Microsoft's FY2024 financials. The analysis will evaluate performance, profitability, efficiency, leverage, liquidity, and DuPont decomposition to move beyond raw financial statement numbers toward a structured, decision-grade view of the company's financial health. Findings will directly inform capital allocation discussions, investor communications, and peer benchmarking.

---

## Company Background & Objectives

Microsoft Corporation (NASDAQ: MSFT) is a global technology company headquartered in Redmond, Washington. Its three segments — Productivity & Business Processes, Intelligent Cloud, and More Personal Computing — generated $245 billion in revenue in FY2024, making it one of the largest companies by market capitalization in the world.

This analysis will answer three core questions:

- Are Microsoft's margins expanding or contracting relative to its own history?
- Is the balance sheet becoming more or less efficient as Azure scales?
- What is the true driver of shareholder returns — margin improvement, asset turnover, or leverage?

---

## Approach

The analysis will use Microsoft's audited FY2024 10-K filing from SEC EDGAR, supplemented by the Microsoft Investor Relations portal and Yahoo Finance for period comparisons. Six ratio categories will be computed from the consolidated income statement, balance sheet, and cash flow statement:

| Category | What It Measures |
|---|---|
| **Performance** | Overall operational effectiveness — revenue growth, asset utilization, and return on invested capital. |
| **Profitability** | Margin quality at each income statement level, revealing pricing power and cost discipline. |
| **Efficiency** | How effectively Microsoft converts assets and working capital into revenue. |
| **Leverage** | Degree of financial risk and debt capacity; signals sustainability of the capital structure. |
| **Liquidity** | Short-term solvency and ability to meet near-term obligations without disrupting operations. |
| **DuPont Analysis** | Decomposes return on equity to isolate whether returns are driven by margin, turnover, or leverage. |

**Data Sources:** SEC EDGAR 10-K (primary audited source) · Microsoft Investor Relations (segment disclosures) · Yahoo Finance / Bloomberg (peer benchmarking against Alphabet, Amazon, and Salesforce)

## Next Steps

**Stage 2 — Excel Model Build:** Construct a dynamic workbook that computes all six ratio categories using real figures pulled directly from Microsoft's FY2024 10-K. Formulas will be labelled and linked to source data for full auditability.

**Stage 3 — Technical Specification:** Document the model's structure, formula logic, and data validation rules. Record key design decisions and lessons learned so the model can be reviewed, reproduced, or handed off.

**Stage 4 — Final Analysis Memo:** Synthesize results into a strategic narrative — covering trend commentary, peer benchmarking, and executive recommendations — alongside a structured AI prompt template for future ratio analyses.
